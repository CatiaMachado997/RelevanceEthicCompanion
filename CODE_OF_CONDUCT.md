# Code of Conduct

This is a project framed around ethics. The standards we hold for the code
apply equally to how we treat each other.

## Our standard

We commit to making participation in this project a welcoming experience
for everyone — regardless of age, body size, visible or invisible
disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender identity and
expression, level of experience, education, socio-economic status,
nationality, personal appearance, race, religion, or sexual identity and
orientation.

We aim to act and interact in ways that contribute to an open, welcoming,
diverse, inclusive, and healthy community.

## What we expect

- Use welcoming and inclusive language.
- Respect differing viewpoints and lived experiences.
- Accept constructive criticism gracefully and offer it kindly.
- Focus on what's best for the project and the community, not for
  yourself.
- Assume good faith when reading a message you're tempted to read as
  hostile.

## What we don't tolerate

- Harassment of any kind, public or private.
- Sexualized language or imagery.
- Trolling, insulting comments, personal or political attacks.
- Publishing others' private information (physical address, email,
  anything else) without their explicit permission.
- Conduct which could reasonably be considered inappropriate in a
  professional setting.

## Enforcement

Project maintainers are responsible for clarifying and enforcing these
standards. They will respond to reported behavior they consider
inappropriate, threatening, offensive, or harmful with whatever action
they judge necessary — including warning, temporary ban, or permanent ban
from project spaces.

Examples of project spaces: this repository, its issue tracker, its pull
requests, any discussion forums or chat channels associated with the
project, and any official communication channels (email, social media)
where someone is acting as a project representative.

## Reporting

If you experience or witness behavior that violates this code, please
report it to the project maintainer privately. For security-related
incidents (e.g. account takeover, malicious PR), see
[`SECURITY.md`](SECURITY.md) instead — that path goes through a private
channel.

For everything else, open a private channel with the maintainer rather
than a public issue. We take confidentiality seriously and will keep
your identity private unless you choose to share it.

## Scope

This code applies in all project spaces, and also when an individual is
officially representing the project in public spaces — e.g. when posting
via an official social media account, when speaking at an online or
offline event as a project representative, or when contributing under
the project's name.
